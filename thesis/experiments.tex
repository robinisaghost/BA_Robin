\chapter{Experiments} \label{ch:experiments}
This chapter presents the empirical results of all experimental configurations evaluated across the cohort of 36 patients. Section~\ref{sec:exp:baseline} establishes the baseline performance. Sections~\ref{sec:exp:obj1} through~\ref{sec:exp:obj3} detail the results for each specific research objective. Section~\ref{sec:exp:overall} concludes with a comprehensive comparative summary.

\section{Objective 3: Event-Centric Evaluation} \label{sec:exp:obj3}
% ================================================================
\begin{planned}
    \item Evaluation of the forecast-derived event detectors across tolerance ranges.
    \item Include the $\tau$-Sweep plot to visually track F1 curves across tolerance ranges for all four training strategies (Fig.~\ref{fig:exp:tau_sweep_plot}). Per-architecture breakdowns of all three metrics (F1, Precision, Recall) are shown in Figs.~\ref{fig:exp:tau_sweep_lstm} and~\ref{fig:exp:tau_sweep_patchtst}. The full numerical breakdowns (Precision, Recall, F1, F2) for all configurations are provided in Tables~\ref{tab:app:tau_sweep_lstm} and~\ref{tab:app:tau_sweep_patchtst} (Appendix~\ref{appendix:detailed_metrics}).
    \item Point out the clear difference between the two architectures in the tau sweep: the LSTM curves stay almost flat across all tolerance values, while the PatchTST curves rise steeply as $\tau$ increases. This suggests that the PatchTST produces predictions that have the right shape but the wrong timing, whereas the LSTM struggles with both timing and shape.
    \item Reporting of the direct binary event classifier performance.
    \item Include a plot showing the direct classifier output trace (probabilities vs.\ true events) to highlight the performance inversion between the recurrent model and the instance-normalized transformer.
    \item Report the lead-time analysis for the two classifiers using Fig.~\ref{fig:exp:lead_time}. The median lead time for the LSTM classifier is 5 minutes, and 49.5\% of all true positive predictions fire at the moment when the patient is already in hypoglycaemia. The PatchTST classifier has a median lead time of 15 minutes and a more even distribution of warning times. This means that the LSTM classifier is largely reactive rather than truly predictive.
    \item For a visual comparison of forecast-derived detectors and direct classifiers in Precision--Recall space, see Fig.~\ref{fig:app:pr_paradox} (Appendix~\ref{appendix:detailed_metrics}).
\end{planned}

\section{Overall Comparison} \label{sec:exp:overall}
% ================================================================
\begin{planned}
    \item Descriptive summary of the global metrics across all tested configurations (see Table~\ref{tab:exp:overall}).
    \item Final structural recommendation motivating a decoupled dual-model system. The results show that the best event detection is achieved by the LSTM direct classifier (F2~=~0.463), while the best trajectory quality is achieved by the PatchTST multi-step model (lowest RMSE among PatchTST variants, best forecasting F2). These two strengths come from different architectures, which suggests that a system using both models in combination would outperform any single model on its own.
\end{planned}
